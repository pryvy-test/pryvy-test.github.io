Background

Social media use among kids & teens has gotten completely out of hand, and why should we be surprised? These are powerful tools that we hand to kids with no preparation or guidance.

Anxiety

Social apps have become one big popularity contest amongst kids, which has led to anxiety, compulsive behavior and overall social media obsession.

Sexting

Teen sexting has become an epidemic in America, and beyond the obvious social consequences, this behavior can lead to expulsion or even jail time.

Mature Content

Popular social apps like Instagram and Twitter are riddled with profanity, drug use and explicit images, and they don't come with parental controls.

Bullying

Kids say things online that they would never say face-to-face, and since social media never stops, neither does the bullying.

Strangers

Public social apps leave kids completely exposed to anyone who wants to pry in on their lives, and unfortunately kids tend to over-share and over-trust online.

Permanent Record

Kids say some off-the-wall things, and when they say them on open social networks those comments are shared, archive and can affect their future potential.

Give kids a chance with Pryvy

By using the Pryvy apps as a group, kids can immediately start sharing & chatting with their friends in a protected, private environment. This gives kids the opportunity to learn how to social media responsibly & respectfully, and keeps them from signing up for unsafe alternatives behind their parent's back.

Teach

The best way to keep kids safe online is to teach them how to be responsible digital citizens.

To friend or not to friend?

Making friends online isn't always black and white. Kids need to learn to evaluate every friend request, and should be aware that not everyone is who they say.

Think twice, share once

We all have stupid thoughts, and kids need to learn that not every thought belongs on social media. The consequences of making a wrong move can lead to expulsion, privacy violations or even jail time in the case of tween/teen sexting.

Respect, respect, respect

We see them everywhere: internet trolls and cyber-bullies. Perhaps the next generation can do better than we are if we teach them early and often about speaking respectfully online.

Connect

The Pryvy sharing & messaging apps allow your group to stay connected even when you're not together.

Kids, group leaders and parents can share personal photos, event photos, updates and other news using the Pryvy Social app.

Teach and Engage

Educators & group leaders can use the Pryvy Social app to reinforce recent lessons, and engage kids by asking questions.

Stay up-to-date

With the Pryvy Messages app, kids no longer have to rely on their parents to keep them in the loop about schedule changes or reminders. Kids can also ask questions or send encouragements to each other.

Protect

By giving kids protected social & messaging apps that they can use with their friends, we can keep them from signing up for unsafe alternatives.

Strangers, Scammers & Bullies

When kids & tweens join social networks, they are often targets of scammers and predators. With Pryvy, only parent approved groups or people can contact children.

Profanity & Nudity

Despite their best efforts, traditional social networks are overrun with profanity, nudity, drug use and hateful language. With Pryvy, kids can share with friends without being exposed to mature content.

Privacy

Unlike almost every other social network, photos and thoughts posted on Pryvy can only be seen by approved friends or groups, not strangers and not advertisers. Furthermore, we allow content to be completely deleted - no questions asked.

Features

Parents control contacts

Parents decide who is allowed to contact their child and who is blocked. Parents can approve individual people or entire groups.

Photos & thoughts are private

Everything shared or sent on Pryvy will only ever be seen by approved friends and family - not strangers and not advertisers.

Not a popularity contest

Likes, retweets, followers, friends, comments, favorites... It's dizzying. For kids, this constant popularity contest leads to anxiety, compulsive behavior and social media addiction. With Pryvy, kids can interact with each others posts, but nothing is tallied.

Accessible

The Pryvy apps are available on iOS devices, Android devices, and any device with a Chrome browser.
